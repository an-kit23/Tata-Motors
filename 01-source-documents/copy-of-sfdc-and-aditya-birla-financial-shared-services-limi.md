Salesforce Implementation Services Methodology 

               Salesforce uses the Salesforce Professional Services Methodology ("SPSM") to provide Implementation Services.  The SPSM involves four stages: Define, Design, Deliver, and Deploy. 

              This methodology is adaptable and tailored to meet each customer's unique needs, incorporating both waterfall and agile principles.  The chosen framework depends on the customer's requirements, the nature of the engagement, and SFDC's recommendations on best practices.  During the Define and Design stages, SFDC and Customer will agree on the planned approach, including how scope is managed.

As a part of the Salesforce Professional Services Methodology:

SFDC and Customer will document the desired configured Application functionality as a list of functional needs by user type (“User Stories”).  

Development occurs in a series of regular increments (“Sprints”), which include delivery of the solution components and testing of them as they are built.  

Development reviews are conducted upon completion of each Sprint to measure functional progress and to validate and provide feedback.

On-going User Story prioritization, as mutually agreed in writing (email sufficient), between the Customer Product Owner and SFDC, will dictate the final content of the configured Application. 

Customer Product Owner (as defined in Section 6) may request additional or alternative functionality based on the prioritization of User Stories as they are refined and developed during Delivery of the configured Application.  Consequently, the precise scope of the configured Application cannot be determined at the outset of the Implementation Services and the detailed scope described above may not be completed as part of the Implementation Services.  

Define & Design Stages

Before the Define & Design stages, SFDC prepares a set of risks, assumptions, project goals, objectives, constraints, success metrics, and stakeholders. 

During Define & Design, SFDC starts the project and collaboratively establishes the Customer's goals and targeted business outcomes.  SFDC and Customer will also outline how the project will be managed and the plan for execution, including scope, resources, schedule, budget, and quality testing.  The Define and Design stages establish the high-level architecture necessary to begin the Deliver stage.  SFDC documents the solution's business and technical components so the Customer can provide formal approval.  The Define & Design stage activities often happen concurrently.  If mutually agreed upon, the Deliver stage can begin before the Define and Design stages close.

During the Define & Design stage, SFDC will coordinate the following meetings to occur sequentially.  

Project Team Alignment: SFDC and Customer teams confirm an initial understanding of the project's scope, undertake preliminary planning, align roles, and project tracking and control mechanisms. 

Kickoff Meeting and Methodology Alignment: The teams align on critical elements of SPSM. SFDC facilitates discovery sessions to create the initial backlog of User Stories. 

Technical Architecture and Integration Analysis: SFDC analyzes the technical requirements for the Implementation Services. 

Build Preparation: Finally, the team makes the preparations to begin the solution delivery, including:

Environments are initialized and configured;

Development and code management tracking and management systems are acquired and set up;  

Sufficient mutually agreed User Stories to start development (typically at least enough for two (2) Sprints).

The Deliver stage can begin before the Define & Design stages are fully completed if mutually agreed by the Customer and SFDC in the Planning Documents.

The Deliver stage is where SFDC configures, develops, and tests the Application over a series of development Sprints based on the product backlog of User Stories, Planning Documents, and Design Documents.  During each Sprint, SFDC and Customer select User Stories, finalize design, build and test the functionality, execute test scripts, and obtain acceptance. After the completion of final Customer UAT, SFDC will work with the Customer to confirm the agreement to deploy the configured Application to the production environment.
